Digital Literacy & Online Tools

Master the art of academic and professional research. Below is your complete repository of tools, archives, and databases necessary for drafting high-level corporate and academic reports.

Google Scholar

The world's most accessible search engine for scholarly literature, peer-reviewed papers, and academic books.

Why it matters
Bypasses standard web junk to exclusively surface verified, citable academic research for your reports.
How to use
  • Use quote marks (" ") for exact phrases
  • Use the "Cite" button to auto-generate APA/MLA formats
Explore Tool
JSTOR

A highly curated digital library of academic journals, books, and primary sources spanning multiple disciplines.

Why it matters
The gold standard for deep humanities, history, and social science archives. Crucial for literature reviews.
How to use
  • Look for "Open Access" tags for free downloads
  • Download PDFs directly to your local drive
Explore Tool

The "Boolean" Search Secret

Stop searching like a beginner. Use AND, OR, and NOT in uppercase when searching databases. For example, searching "Artificial Intelligence" AND "Education" NOT "Robotics" will instantly filter out thousands of irrelevant papers.

Search & Discover: Find 1 peer-reviewed research paper on Google Scholar.

Extract: Download 1 full-text article from DOAJ or JSTOR.
