Write with Clarity

Grammarly

An advanced AI writing assistant that reviews spelling, grammar, punctuation, and clarity.

Why it matters
Even brilliant ideas fail if they are poorly communicated. It ensures professional polish.
Use Case
"Checking a final academic essay for tone and passive voice."
How to use
  • Install the browser extension
  • Set your audience to "Academic" or "Formal"
  • Review suggestions, don't just blind-accept
Use Grammarly

Turnitin

The global standard for academic integrity and plagiarism detection.

Why it matters
Plagiarism (even accidental) can ruin an academic career. This ensures your work is 100% original.
Use Case
"Scanning a thesis chapter before university submission."
How to use
  • Upload your final document draft
  • Review the "Similarity Score" report
  • Properly quote or paraphrase flagged sections
Explore Turnitin

Manage Your References

Zotero

A free, open-source tool to help you collect, organize, annotate, cite, and share research.

Why citations matter
They give credit to original authors and allow readers to trace your logical foundation.
Key Features
  • One-click saving from web browsers
  • Auto-generates bibliographies in Word/Docs
  • Organize PDFs into a searchable library
Get Zotero

Mendeley

A reference manager and academic social network that helps you organize your research.

Example Use Case
"Auto-formatting 50+ references into APA 7th Edition style."
Key Features
  • Highlight and annotate PDFs securely
  • Sync library across desktop and web
  • Discover related papers via network
Get Mendeley

Your Task (Day 25)

Project: Mini Research Article

This is it. Compile your research, data, and writing skills into one final document.

  • Step 1: Choose a topic from your internship track.
  • Step 2: Write a 300–500 word draft.
  • Step 3: Insert proper in-text citations.
  • Step 4: Review for plagiarism and grammar.

Scholar's Advice

  • Write clearly, not complex. True intellect is taking a complex topic and making it easy to understand. Avoid jargon salad.
  • Always cite sources. If it is not your original thought, cite it.
  • Revise multiple times. The first draft is just you telling yourself the story. The edit is for the reader.
